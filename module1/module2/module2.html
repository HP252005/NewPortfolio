AD, or Computer-Aided Design, is a technology that utilizes software to create, modify, analyze, or optimize designs for various industries such as engineering, architecture, automotive, aerospace, and more. CAD software often supports parametric modeling, where designs are created using parameters and constraints. Changes to one part of the design automatically update related components, maintaining design integrity and speeding up the iterative process.

CAD allows designers to create assemblies of parts, where individual components can be positioned, constrained, and assembled into larger mechanisms or structures. This is crucial for designing complex systems such as machinery or vehicles.

In Second we learn about how to make resume.A resume is a document that summarizes an individual's education, work experience, skills, achievements, and other relevant information, typically used when applying for jobs.
Contact Information Include your name, address, phone number, and email address at the top of the resume. A brief statement that summarizes your professional experience, skills, and career goals. List your educational background, including degrees earned, institutions attended, and any relevant certifications or honors.

Detail your work history in reverse chronological order, including job titles, the name of the company or organization, dates of employment, and key responsibilities and accomplishments for each position.
Highlight your relevant skills, including technical skills, soft skills, and any specialized knowledge or expertise. Include any notable achievements, awards, or recognitions you have received in your career. Depending on your background and the job you're applying for, you may also include additional sections such as volunteer experience, professional affiliations, publications, or language proficiency.

When creating a resume, it's important to tailor it to the specific job you're applying for, highlighting the skills and experiences most relevant to the position. Additionally, make sure your resume is well-organized, easy to read, and free of errors. A clean, professional-looking format can make a positive impression on potential employers.
